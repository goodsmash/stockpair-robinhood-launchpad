# v0.6.0 security review and exploit closure

## Scope

The review covered the Solidity protocol, browser wallet/write path, runtime rendering, REST/SSE indexer perimeter, deployment validation, local startup and release automation.

## Finding 1 — untrusted indexer data reached dynamic HTML and URLs

**Risk:** a compromised or poisoned indexer could submit malformed addresses, hashes, labels, findings or explorer URLs. TypeScript types do not validate runtime JSON, and incomplete escaping can create DOM or link-injection risk.

**Fix:** all API/SSE objects pass through explicit normalizers with maximum lengths, strict address/hash/decimal parsing, finite integer bounds, risk-enum mapping and bounded evidence arrays. Responses require JSON and remain under 2 MB; stream events remain under 256 KB. External explorer links require safe origins and reject credentials, scripts and control characters.

**Regression:** `apps/web/scripts/security-runtime.test.mjs` exercises the actual helper implementation; `apps/web/scripts/ui-contract.test.mjs` asserts normalization and write/render invariants.

## Finding 2 — compromised frontend could choose unsafe deadlines and minimums

**Risk:** valid contracts could receive effectively long-lived transactions or near-zero output/liquidity minimums from a modified interface.

**Fix:** `StockCoinLaunchpad` and `StockCoinPool` enforce a 30-minute deadline maximum. `StockCoinPool` requires swap minimums within 3% of the current quote and liquidity minimums within 1% of the preview.

**Regression:** `scripts/e2e/frontend-compromise.test.mjs` proves 31-minute deadlines and loose minima revert while bounded calls succeed.

## Finding 3 — eligibility and ownership recovery was too immediate

**Risk:** compromised ownership could immediately replace eligibility trust roles or clear an emergency denial; pending ownership acceptance did not expire.

**Fix:** pending ownership expires after seven days. Eligibility validity is capped at 30 days. Attestor/guardian changes and emergency-denial clearing use an exact-action 48-hour delay with a seven-day execution window and guardian cancellation. Revocation and emergency denial remain immediate.

**Regression:** `scripts/e2e/eligibility-governance.test.mjs` proves immediate changes fail, cancellation works, eligibility duration is bounded and ownership acceptance expires.

## Finding 4 — approval/provenance and review-dialog weaknesses

**Risk:** non-exact allowances and incomplete pool evidence increase the impact of a compromised frontend. In addition, transaction and deployer dialogs did not fully contain keyboard focus or restore focus, creating poor and potentially confusing signature-review UX.

**Fix:** exact allowance equality, zero-first replacement, best-effort revocation, full direct pool provenance, labelled dialogs, focus containment, Escape handling, previous-focus restoration, mobile scroll constraints and visible focus indicators.

**Regression:** browser contract tests assert exact allowances, full pool provenance symbols, active-navigation state and keyboard-modal behavior.

## Defense in depth

- direct RPC evidence remains authoritative for writes;
- indexer data can degrade discovery but cannot authorize a spender;
- exact chain/factory/hash/version trust anchors;
- right-to-left trusted-proxy parsing;
- consistent global/per-IP SSE accounting and slow-client eviction;
- exact production origins without credentials/path/query/fragment for indexer and explorer bases;
- source-matched compiled artifacts and generated browser ABI; and
- public operations disabled by default.

## Residual risk

No local review proves absence of unknown bugs. Third-party wallets, RPCs, explorers, DNS/CDN/CI, hosting accounts, canonical stock assets, oracles, eligibility providers and legal status remain outside the demonstrated boundary. Independent audits and real target-chain staging are mandatory before real-value use.
